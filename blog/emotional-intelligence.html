What is Emotional Intelligence?

It is a set of emotional and social skills that influence the way we build and maintain social relationships and cope with challenges. However, Emotional Intelligence can be improved and strengthened.

    The attributes that defines the EQ:
    Self-management: One can manage one's emotion. Therefore, he/she is not likely to have impulsive feelings and behaviours but rather take initiative, follow through on commitments, and adapt to chaning circumstances.
    Self-awareness: One awares one's strengths and weaknesses and have self-confidence. One is able to diagnose how emotions affect thoughts and behaviours.
    Social-awareness: Similar to self-awareness but toward the others. Therefore, empathy is important in this attribute. One can pick up other's emotions, needs, concerns, and emotional cues. They also feel comfortable socially.
    Relationship management: One knows how to develop and maintain good relationships. The skills might include ability to manage conflict, work as a team, inspire and influence, and communicate cleary.

How is it different to IQ?

Emotional Intelligence generally refers to the ability to sense emotion in oneself and in other people. It also refers to how one uses that awareness to guide one's behaviour. However, IQ usually refers to the intellectual ability such as using logic to problem solving, planning and strategising, understanding abstract ideas, and adapting to change etc

Why is it important?

According to the scientific research, our emotions precedes our thoughts. It means when the emotions run high, it changes the way our brain functions. It diminishes our cognitive activities eg decision-making and interpersonal skills. Therefore, understanding how EQ influence ourselves is important as it can help us to be more successful in our lives.
